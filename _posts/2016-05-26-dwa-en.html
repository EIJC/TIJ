Petra Kohnen from Deutschen Welle Akademie at the meeting in May 2016

We were happy to welcome as guest Petra Kohnen from Deutschen Welle Akademie. (centre)

 

 

Foto: EIJK

She discussed current the journalist's projects and asked for their expactations: “We would like to hear about German media and Deutsche Welle Academy, trainings and jobs.“

 

Media law, networks and marketing - Tarek Khello knows that he has to improve his knowledge. He works as freelancer for a German public broadcaster.

Foto: EIJK

 

Ola Aljari reported onher research for the European Centre for Press & Media Freedom (ECPMF) on reportin on refugees in Greece.

Foto: EIJK

 

Syrian publisher Tarek Aziza (left) introduced his recent project: He works for the first bilingual newspaper for refugges: Abwab. The team wants to give information on current public discussions in Germany - and tries to help refugees to find their way in Germany.

Foto: EIJK

 

The newspaper started in December 2015. It is a free monthly newspaper and distributed also in refugee camps. Additionally an online version exist, but no App.

Foto: EIJK

 

“Mobile Reporting - that's the future” says Petra Kohnen (DWA). Everybody has a smartphone - and you just need one by yourselve to publish good news and stories. And it is easy to make money of it.

Foto: EIJK

 

Deutsche Welle Academy and Engagement Global provide special workshops for journalists from the global south at the conference “Bildkorrekturen”. Petra Kohnen also introduced trainings at the Academy to the journalists.

Foto: EIJK

 

We would like to thank Sabine Witt from Engagement Global (left) for this great evening, as well as Jane Whyatt, project manager of ECPMF for the support.
